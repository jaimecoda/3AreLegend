Active 2-Day Escapes

Tailored for 3 Active Men • Departing Boston by Car

TL;DR High-impact, multi-option 2-day itineraries combining intense mountain or coastal terrain with distinct regional culinary and brewery outposts.
Drive Time from Boston: ~2.5 to 3 hours
Primary Route: I-93 N through Franconia Notch
Passes: US Forest Service Pass ($5/day at trailheads)

Day 1: Arrival & High-Impact Hiking

Mount Willard (Crawford Notch)
A mid-difficulty 3.2-mile trail scaling roughly 900 vertical feet. Delivers an abrupt, dramatic cliff face overview looking straight down the massive glaciated pass.

Day 1 Evening: Trail Recovery

Ledge Brewing Company (Intervale, NH)
An expansive taproom and outdoor space engineered directly for hikers. Features robust house-brewed New England ales and on-site scratch kitchens or local food trucks.

Day 2: Alpine Summits or River Lines

Mount Washington Summit Road
Ascend the steep, technical 7.6-mile mountain auto road straight into the extreme conditions zone at the 6,288-foot peak for unmatched regional vantage points.

Drive Time from Boston: ~4.5 to 5 hours
Primary Route: I-95 N to ME-3 E
Passes: Acadia National Park Vehicle Pass ($35). Advanced online reservation mandatory for Cadillac Mountain.

Day 1: Iron Rungs & Ocean Cliffs

The Precipice (or Beehive) Trail
An intense, exposed vertical climb utilizing embedded iron ladders, rungs, and narrow granite shelves. Delivers sheer drops directly over the open ocean waves below.

Day 1 Evening: Coastal Provisions

Atlantic Brewing Midtown (Bar Harbor)
Sample house-made local specialties like Coal Porter or blueberry ales alongside fresh wood-fired provisions or wharf-side steamed lobster setups.

Day 2 Morning: Early Peaks & Marine Lines

Frenchman Bay Kayaking or Sand Beach
