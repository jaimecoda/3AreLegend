The Legends' Epic Roadtrip

Tailored for the 3 Are Legend • Departing Boston by Car

Summary
Compare these high-impact, multi-option 2-day itineraries to decide which ultimate roadtrip adventure to choose for our friend group. Select between the rugged, high-alpine peaks of New Hampshire's White Mountains or the dramatic granite cliffs and ocean views of Acadia National Park, complete with custom routes, hikes, and local craft breweries.
Drive Time from Boston: ~2.5 to 3 hours
Primary Route: I-93 N through Franconia Notch
Passes: US Forest Service Pass ($5/day at trailheads)
Where to Sleep: Notchland Inn (Crawford Notch trailside historic lodge) or Attitash Mountain Village (Bartlett cabins/townhouses)

Day 1: Arrival & High-Impact Hiking

Drive: Boston to Crawford Notch, NH
⏱️ Estimated Duration: 2.5–3 hours
Load up the car and depart Boston early via I-93 N, transitioning from the urban sprawl into the scenic mountain highway winding through Franconia Notch. The route takes you directly into the heart of the White Mountains, offering incredible views of steep granite peaks right from the road.

Mount Willard (Crawford Notch)
⏱️ Estimated Duration: 2–3 hours
A mid-difficulty 3.2-mile out-and-back trail starting from the historic Crawford Depot. The trail ascends steadily through scenic northern hardwood forests before reaching the steep, rocky ledges at the summit. Scaling roughly 900 vertical feet, it delivers an abrupt, breathtaking cliff face overview looking straight down the center of the massive, U-shaped glaciated pass of Crawford Notch. For those seeking an even higher-intensity challenge, the Franconia Ridge Loop (9-mile strenuous alpine trek) serves as an alternative.

Ledge Brewing Company (Intervale, NH)
⏱️ Estimated Duration: 2–3 hours
An expansive, welcoming taproom and outdoor beer garden in Intervale, NH, engineered specifically for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts. Sample flights of fresh, house-brewed New England IPAs, stouts, and crisp lagers while relaxing by the outdoor fire pits. The venue regularly features on-site scratch kitchens, local food trucks, and live music, making it the perfect location to swap trail stories and recover from the day's physical exertion. Alternatively, Moat Mountain Smoke House in North Conway offers craft beers and slow-smoked BBQ.

Day 2: Mount Washington or Saco River Lines

Alternative A: Alpine Summit
Mount Washington Summit Road
⏱️ Estimated Duration: 2–3 hours
Drive the steep, winding, and technical 7.6-mile Mount Washington Auto Road to reach the 6,288-foot summit, the highest peak in the Northeast. Expect a thrilling, high-focus drive through three distinct climate zones, culminating in extreme alpine weather conditions and panoramic vistas stretching across five states and into Canada. Explore the historic Tip-Top House and the Sherman Adams Visitor Center at the peak.

Alternative B: River Lines
Saco River Open-Top Kayaking
⏱️ Estimated Duration: 3–4 hours
Enjoy a scenic, active-recovery kayaking run down the Saco River in Conway, NH. This open-top kayaking activity features a gentle current, sandy beaches for swimming and resting, and beautiful mountain valley scenery. Rent a kayak locally and drift along clean, cold waters under a pine canopy.

Day 1: Arrival & High-Impact Hiking

Drive: Boston to Bar Harbor, ME
⏱️ Estimated Duration: 4.5–5 hours
Load up the car and depart Boston early via I-95 N and ME-3 E, transitioning from the Massachusetts highways to the gorgeous rocky coast of Maine. You will cross the bridge onto Mount Desert Island and arrive in the historic, seaside downtown of Bar Harbor.
Open in Google Maps
Alternative A: Extreme Cliff Climb
The Precipice (or Beehive) Trail
⏱️ Estimated Duration: 2.5–3.5 hours (Precipice) or 1.5–2 hours (Beehive)
A strenuous, highly exposed 1.6-mile loop climbing 1,000 vertical feet up the sheer east face of Champlain Mountain. Using embedded iron ladders, rungs, handholds, and granite shelves, this non-technical climbing route is not for the faint of heart. It features extreme cliff-side exposure and rewards climbers with stunning panoramic views of the open ocean. If the Precipice is closed for peregrine falcon nesting (typically late spring to mid-summer), the Beehive Trail serves as an excellent fallback.

Alternative B: Scenic Ridge Hike
Gorham Mountain Trail (via Cadillac Cliffs)
⏱️ Estimated Duration: 1.5–2.5 hours
A highly scenic, moderate 1.8-mile loop climbing 525 vertical feet. The trail ascends through pitch pine forests to a wide-open granite ridge offering spectacular panoramic views of Sand Beach, Great Head, and the open ocean. To add some light adventure, take the Cadillac Cliffs side trail to walk through narrow rock tunnels and caverns, avoiding the high exposure of the Precipice.

Atlantic Brewing Midtown (Bar Harbor)
⏱️ Estimated Duration: 2–3 hours
A prime downtown Bar Harbor taproom offering a rotating selection of local Maine craft brews, including their famous Bar Harbor Blueberry Ale and rich Coal Porter. The midtown location provides a lively atmosphere perfect for refueling after a demanding day of climbing. Pair your beers with wood-fired pizzas and fresh local provisions, or head down to the nearby waterfront lobster wharfs for classic, freshly steamed Maine lobster rolls and local seafood.

Day 2: Frenchman Bay Kayaking or Sand Beach Surf

Alternative A: Marine Excursion
Frenchman Bay Guided Sea Kayaking
⏱️ Estimated Duration: 3 hours
Execute a fast-paced morning guided ocean sea kayaking excursion in Frenchman Bay, paddling around the rocky Porcupine Islands to view seals, nesting eagles, and dramatic granite headlands. Learn about the bay's marine ecology while enjoying an active coastal paddle.

Take an invigorating morning cold-plunge surf swim in the 55°F Atlantic waters at the iconic Sand Beach. Nestled between massive pink granite headlands, this cold plunge offers an intense physical shock and active-recovery swim in the crashing ocean surf.
